Company Profile

NordHav at a Glance

Founded in 2003 by three Norwegian fishing families, NordHav has grown from 3 licenses into one of Norway's leading vertically integrated salmon producers.

🏢 Corporate Identity

  • Legal Name: NordHav Aquaculture AS
  • Org.nr: 987 654 321
  • Headquarters: Bontelabo 2, 5003 Bergen, Norway
  • Structure: Single legal entity (no subsidiaries)
  • CIO: Lars Erik Henriksen
  • CEO: Marianne Dahl (since 2018)

📊 Ownership

  • Henriksen Family — 38% (via Henriksen Holding AS)
  • Solberg Family — 32% (via Solberg Invest AS)
  • Vikanes Family — 22% (via Vikanes Maritime AS)
  • Management & Employees — 8% (employee share program)

Privately held. Board of 5 members including 2 independents.

🎯 Strategic Priorities 2026–2030

  1. Volume Growth: Increase harvest to 75,000 tonnes GWE by 2030 (+44%) through improved biology and post-smolt strategies.
  2. VAP Expansion: Double VAP output to capture more value per kilogram — focus on premium convenience products for EU and Asian retail.
  3. Sustainability Leadership: Science-based emission targets (SBTi), 100% ASC certification, FCR below 1.10.
  4. Digital Transformation: Replace legacy systems with unified ERP, IoT-driven precision aquaculture, predictive analytics. This ERP project is the cornerstone.
  5. Market Diversification: Expand North American and Asia-Pacific presence while maintaining European core.

Project Objectives

What NordHav Needs From This ERP

🎯 Primary Objectives

  1. Unified Platform: Replace 12+ fragmented systems with a single integrated ERP across all locations and business functions.
  2. End-to-End Traceability: Full traceability from egg/smolt → sea → harvest → processing → delivery, meeting ASC/BRC/GlobalG.A.P./regulatory requirements.
  3. Operational Efficiency: Eliminate manual data entry, spreadsheet planning, and paper workflows. Enable real-time cross-functional visibility.
  4. Regulatory Compliance: SAF-T, MVA, grunnrenteskatt, A-melding, BarentsWatch lice reporting, food safety — all automated, all audit-ready.
  5. Scalability: Support growth from 52k → 75k tonnes GWE and VAP doubling without architecture changes.
  6. Data-driven Decisions: Real-time dashboards and analytics: biomass, FCR, lice, generation P&L, cost-per-kg, cash flow.
  7. Industry Fit: Aquaculture-specific functionality for fish farming, seafood processing, cold-chain logistics, and biological asset accounting.

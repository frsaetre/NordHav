NordHav at a Glance

🎯 Strategic Priorities 2026–2030

  1. Volume Growth: Increase harvest to 75,000 tonnes GWE by 2030 (+44%) through improved biology and post-smolt strategies.
  2. VAP Expansion: Double VAP output to capture more value per kilogram — focus on premium convenience products for EU and Asian retail.
  3. Sustainability Leadership: Science-based emission targets (SBTi), 100% ASC certification, FCR below 1.10.
  4. Digital Transformation: Replace legacy systems with unified ERP, IoT-driven precision aquaculture, predictive analytics. This ERP project is the cornerstone.
  5. Market Diversification: Expand North American and European presence through newly established sales offices in Boston (USA) and Rotterdam (Netherlands), enabled by the new multi-entity ERP platform.
History

Company Timeline

From 3 sea-water licenses to one of Norway's leading vertically integrated salmon producers.

2003
Founded by the Henriksen, Solberg, and Vikanes families with 3 sea-water licenses in Hordaland and a single smolt facility in Osterøy. Initial harvest ~5,000t GWE.
2005
Acquired processing facility in Austevoll, beginning the vertical integration strategy with HOG and fillet products.
2008–2011
Acquired 14 additional sea licenses across Sogn og Fjordane and Møre og Romsdal. Built state-of-the-art RAS smolt facility in Masfjorden (12M smolt/year capacity).
2012–2013
Doubled Austevoll processing capacity to 150t/day. Opened dedicated VAP facility producing smoked salmon, marinated products, and meal kits.
2014–2015
Secured 5 licenses in Nordland county. Harvest volumes reached 35,000t GWE. Revenue exceeded NOK 2 billion.
2016–2019
Achieved ASC and GlobalG.A.P. certifications. Deployed precision feeding technology. Reduced pharmaceutical lice treatments by 70%. FSSC 22000 for processing.
2020–2022
Second RAS facility built in Kvam (8M smolt). Post-smolt facility commissioned in Masfjorden (grow-out to 500g). Launched direct-to-foodservice channel.
2025
52,000t GWE, NOK 4.2B revenue, 820 employees. Digital transformation initiative launched — the ERP project begins.
2026 — Today
Dynamics 365 F&O + AquaMonitor platform goes live. The unified ERP enables NordHav to establish its first international subsidiaries — NordHav USA Inc. (Boston, MA) and NordHav Nederland B.V. (Rotterdam) — with dedicated sales offices serving the North American and European markets directly.

Industry Context

Norwegian Salmon Farming Industry

Norway produces ~50% of the world's farmed Atlantic salmon. A heavily regulated, technology-intensive industry generating NOK 120B+ in annual exports.

🌍 Market Overview

  • Global share: Norway = ~50% of world farmed Atlantic salmon
  • Annual exports: NOK 120+ billion to 100+ countries
  • Demand drivers: Health consciousness, protein shift, convenience trends
  • Price dynamics: Significant spot price volatility; 30–50% sold on fixed contracts
  • Sustainability: Increasing retailer/consumer demand for ASC and organic certification
  • Traceability: Full chain-of-custody from egg to plate required by regulation and customers

🏛️ Regulatory Framework

  • Fiskeridirektoratet: Production licenses, MAB regulation, volume monitoring
  • Mattilsynet: Fish health, welfare, food safety, pharmaceutical controls
  • Miljødirektoratet: Environmental impact, emissions, waste management
  • Statsforvalteren: Regional environmental monitoring and compliance
  • Key legislation: Akvakulturloven, Sea Lice Regulation, NYTEK standards, IK-Akvakultur
  • Financial: SAF-T, MVA, Grunnrenteskatt (25% resource rent tax from 2024)

🐟 Production Cycle — Egg to Harvest (2.5–3.5 years)

1
Broodstock & Roe

Month 0–2. Eggs stripped, fertilized, incubated at controlled temp.

2
Hatchery (Fry)

Month 2–6. Alevins absorb yolk sac → fry at first-feeding.

3
Parr

Month 6–12. Grading, vaccination. RAS can accelerate to 10–12 months.

4
Post-Smolt

Month 12–20. Land-based grow-out to 200–500g before sea transfer.

5
Sea Grow-out

Month 16–36. Net pens, feed mgmt, lice control to 4–6 kg harvest weight.

6
Harvest

Starve 48–72h, well-boat transport, humane slaughter at processing plant.
